Fortunately, over the past few decades cognitive science has begun to free itself from the conceptual shackles of dualism and to treat human thought as fundamentally “embodied.”
The embodied view of cognition also views thought as inherently tied to feeling, which calls into question any rigid distinction between rationality and emotion.
